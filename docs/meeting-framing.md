# Institutional Meeting Framing

## Opening

This is an independent, fictional pilot showing how an institution-approved language model could support structured Socratic assessment while faculty retain academic authority.

## Core distinction

The project does not attempt to replace or compete with an institution's AI service. The AI supplies language capability. The Socratic Platform supplies the educational architecture: learning objectives, pedagogical moves, learner-state tracking, evidence provenance, stopping conditions, and faculty review.

## Current readiness

The project is ready for architectural and pilot-design discussion, not production deployment. The next institutional questions concern educational fit, approved provider access, privacy, hosting, authentication, accessibility, and the definition of a controlled pilot.

## Suggested formulation

> I have separated the project into a private reasoning core, a public educational showcase, and this fictional institutional pilot. I would like to validate the educational workflow with General Education leadership and understand how an institution-approved AI service could connect through a narrow provider interface. The goal is not another chatbot; it is a faculty-governed assessment process that produces reviewable evidence of learner understanding.
