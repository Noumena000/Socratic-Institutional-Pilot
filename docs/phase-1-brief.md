# Phase 1 Functional Demonstration Brief

## Goal

Turn the Phase 0 architecture into one polished, end-to-end fictional assessment experience.

## Required interaction

1. Display a faculty-defined prompt and evidence target.
2. Accept or reveal a fictional learner response.
3. Show a structured interpretation of the response.
4. Identify the unmet evidence need.
5. Show the pedagogical move selected by the educational layer.
6. Distinguish that decision from the AI provider's phrasing function.
7. Advance the dialogue through at least one follow-up response.
8. Update the understanding map and evidence timeline.
9. Produce a faculty-facing evidence portfolio with transcript references.
10. End with an explicit human-review decision point.

## Boundaries

- fictional data only;
- no production provider connection;
- no credentials or client-side API keys;
- no Nightingale or Florence branding;
- no automatic grade or misconduct determination;
- no disclosure of the private reasoning implementation.

## Demonstration quality criteria

The completed experience should look like an institutional assessment product rather than a chatbot. The conversation is one stage of a larger workflow that begins with curriculum and ends with faculty judgment.
