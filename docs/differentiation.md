# Why an Institutional Platform?

## Central thesis

Institutions do not merely need access to a capable language model. They need an educational system that turns language-model capability into a repeatable, governed, faculty-reviewable assessment process.

> Institution-approved AI provides language intelligence. The Socratic Platform provides educational intelligence.

The two layers are complementary. The platform is designed to make an approved AI service more educationally useful without making the model itself the assessment authority.

## General-purpose AI and the Socratic Platform

| General-purpose or institution-approved AI | Socratic institutional platform |
| --- | --- |
| Generates and interprets language | Conducts a structured assessment workflow |
| Responds to prompts | Selects pedagogical moves against explicit evidence needs |
| Explains concepts | Tracks what the learner has and has not demonstrated |
| Supports open-ended conversation | Maintains a bounded dialogue with stopping conditions |
| Produces summaries | Produces transcript-linked evidence for faculty review |
| Depends on the selected model | Preserves the educational workflow across providers |
| Can assist learners | Supports faculty judgment without replacing it |

This is not a claim that a language model cannot imitate parts of the workflow when carefully prompted. It is a claim that an institution needs the workflow to be explicit, repeatable, auditable, curriculum-aligned, and governed independently of any one model.

## The differentiated capability

The platform adds four layers that an AI service alone does not provide as a complete institutional product:

1. **Pedagogical policy** — determines which Socratic move is appropriate and why.
2. **Learner-state modeling** — records demonstrated, tentative, contradicted, and revised understanding across turns.
3. **Evidence architecture** — links claims about understanding to specific learner responses.
4. **Faculty governance** — keeps academic decisions, interpretation, and intervention under human authority.

## Provider independence

The educational workflow should remain stable if the institution changes providers. A local model, a commercial API, or an institution-owned service may supply bounded language functions through the same provider contract.

The provider may help:

- interpret natural-language responses;
- extract claims, reasons, distinctions, and objections;
- phrase an already-selected pedagogical move;
- summarize transcript-grounded evidence.

The provider should not independently determine:

- final grades;
- misconduct findings;
- institutional policy;
- whether sufficient evidence exists without the platform's policy layer;
- what learning objectives matter.

## Meeting formulation

> This is not another chatbot. It is a provider-independent educational intelligence layer that uses an institution-approved language model to conduct structured Socratic dialogue and produce reviewable evidence of learner understanding.
