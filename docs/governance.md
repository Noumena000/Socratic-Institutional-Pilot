# Governance Principles

## Faculty authority

The platform produces evidence for faculty review. It does not make final academic, grading, or misconduct decisions.

## Evidence before inference

Every instructor-facing claim should be traceable to a learner turn, target concept, and recorded reasoning event.

## Transparent uncertainty

The system should distinguish demonstrated understanding, tentative understanding, unresolved gaps, and possible misconceptions. Confidence labels must not be presented as certainty.

## Data minimization

A production pilot should collect only the data required for the approved educational purpose, define retention and deletion periods, and restrict access by role.

## Provider independence

Institution-approved AI services should connect through a replaceable adapter. Provider selection must not determine the pedagogical model.

## Human review

Automated outputs must be reviewable, correctable, and contestable. Learners and faculty should be able to understand what evidence informed a conclusion.

## No AI-authorship detection

The platform evaluates visible reasoning in the dialogue. It is not an AI detector and must not infer misconduct from writing style, probability scores, or unsupported signals.

## Accessibility and fairness

Pilot evaluation should include accessibility, disparate-impact review, usability testing, and alternate pathways for learners who require accommodations.
