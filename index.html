The central distinction

Language intelligence generates language. Educational intelligence governs learning.

Socratic educational layer

Defines what evidence matters, identifies what remains missing, chooses the next instructional move, and preserves faculty authority.

Why not just use a chatbot?

A model can converse. An institution needs curriculum alignment, evidence provenance, and governance.

The platform does not compete with an approved AI service. It gives that service a repeatable educational role.

Language model

  • Responds to prompts
  • Generates natural-language dialogue
  • Explains and summarizes content
  • May change when providers change

Educational intelligence platform

  • Begins with learning objectives
  • Defines evidence requirements
  • Tracks learner state over time
  • Explains why each question was selected
  • Keeps the workflow stable across providers

The product is not the chatbot. The product is the educational decision system surrounding it.

Fictional module · Week 6

Utilitarianism and consequentialist reasoning

Learners examine whether the moral value of an action depends primarily on its consequences and apply Utilitarian reasoning to a professional dilemma.

Estimated dialogue: 8 minutes

Learning objectives

  1. Explain the foundational principles of Utilitarianism.
  2. Distinguish consequentialist reasoning from intention- or rule-centered approaches.
  3. Apply Utilitarian reasoning to a novel professional case.
  4. Evaluate an objection concerning justice or individual rights.
  5. Defend or revise an initial judgment after considering the objection.

Assessment role

Evidence through dialogue

The dialogue is not a quiz replacement or automatic grade. It gathers evidence for faculty review.

Evidence requirements

  • DefinitionExplains utility and consequences.
  • ApplicationUses the framework in a novel case.
  • ObjectionEngages justice or rights.
  • RevisionDefends or qualifies the initial judgment.

Common misconceptions

  • “Greatest number” always equals greatest overall utility.
  • Any beneficial outcome justifies any means.
  • Utilitarianism ignores impartial comparison.

Pedagogical policy

  • Clarify before challenging.
  • Request application before evaluating transfer.
  • Introduce an objection only after the theory is represented fairly.
  • Stop when the required evidence is sufficient for faculty review.

Stopping conditions

Prepare the evidence portfolio when the learner has explained the principle, applied it to the case, engaged a rights or justice objection, and either revised or explicitly defended the conclusion.
