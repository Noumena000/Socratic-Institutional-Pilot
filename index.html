The central distinction

Language intelligence is not the same as educational intelligence.

01

Institution-approved AI

Interprets language, generates explanations, phrases questions, and summarizes bounded information.

02

Socratic educational layer

Defines the evidence need, selects the pedagogical move, tracks learner state, and preserves faculty authority.

Why not just use a chatbot?

A capable model can converse. An institution needs a repeatable and governed assessment process.

This distinction does not diminish the value of institutional AI. The platform makes that service more educationally useful by surrounding it with curriculum, policy, evidence, and review.

General or institution-approved AI

  • Generates and interprets language
  • Responds to an individual prompt
  • Supports open-ended conversation
  • Explains or summarizes content
  • Depends on the selected model

Socratic institutional platform

  • Operates against explicit learning objectives
  • Selects pedagogical moves from unmet evidence needs
  • Tracks understanding, contradiction, and revision
  • Links claims about learning to transcript evidence
  • Preserves the workflow across AI providers

This is not another chatbot. It is a provider-independent educational intelligence layer for structured dialogue, evidence modeling, and faculty-guided judgment.

Provider independent by design

The model is a component. The educational workflow is the product.
